# Reviving a C# scraper in 2026: narrow yes, broad no

**Verdict up front: do not revive a general-purpose C# scraping library as an open-source mindshare play. Python has won that race and the gap is widening, not closing.** [high-confidence] A focused, opinionated, paid-or-dual-licensed product targeting .NET enterprises around three specific pillars (AOT-clean pipeline, MCP-native exposure, real anti-bot primitives, especially TLS/HTTP-2 fingerprint control) has a defensible niche worth pursuing. Without commitment to the hard engineering, the revival becomes a coat of paint on top of AngleSharp plus Playwright .NET, which already cover roughly 80% of enterprise needs. The realistic outcome envelope is a $1 to $3M ARR consultancy-anchored business over 3 to 5 years, not a venture-scale outcome. The rest of this report justifies that verdict and gives you the technical and commercial map.

A note on the premise. You framed this as "is the ship sailing?" The honest answer is more nuanced: **the ship sailed for the category you probably remember (hand-rolled selector libraries fighting Cloudflare on developer ingenuity).** A different ship is leaving the harbor (agent-callable, MCP-fronted, LLM-extracting scraping infrastructure integrated into enterprise AI stacks), and that one has open berths in .NET specifically.

## What AI actually changed about extraction

The dominant production pattern in 2026 is **schema-driven extraction with deterministic-first, LLM-fallback routing**, not LLM-first extraction. [high-confidence, convergent across Zyte, Crawl4AI, Firecrawl, ScrapeGraphAI primary sources] Every serious tool (Firecrawl /extract, Crawl4AI's `LLMExtractionStrategy`, ScrapeGraphAI, Jina ReaderLM-v2, AgentQL, Diffbot, Zyte custom attributes, Apify Actor schemas) converges on the same contract: user provides a Pydantic or JSON schema, system returns validated structured data. The debate is which substrate satisfies the contract most cheaply.

The cost math is decisive. A deterministic CSS/XPath selector pass is effectively free (microseconds, no API cost). An LLM extraction on a 4K-token cleaned Markdown page costs roughly **$0.0004 with Gemini 2.0 Flash or GPT-5 nano, $0.001 to $0.0015 with GPT-4o-mini class, $0.005 to $0.01 with Claude Haiku class, $0.02 to $0.05 with frontier models, $0.01 to $0.05 with vision models on screenshots.** Managed extraction APIs (Firecrawl /extract, ScrapeGraphAI hosted) carry a 5x to 30x markup over direct LLM calls. [high-confidence inference from published pricing] At 10 million pages per month against a known site, the gap between selectors and LLMs is $0 versus $4,000 to $50,000.

So the question "are Scrapy, BeautifulSoup, Playwright, HtmlAgilityPack, AngleSharp obsolete?" has a clean answer: **no, they are the substrate every AI tool builds on.** [verified] Crawl4AI is Playwright plus custom Markdown plus optional LLM. Firecrawl runs Chromium under the hood. ScrapeGraphAI uses Playwright and BeautifulSoup. Zyte's published architecture explicitly blends Scrapy, supervised ML, and LLM, picking per field by cost and accuracy. The shift is not "replace lxml" but **"lxml or AngleSharp produces the substrate; LLM does the last-mile semantic mapping when selectors fail."** The Crawl4AI `generate_schema` pattern, Reworkd's whole product, and ScrapeGraphAI's `ScriptCreatorGraph` formalize this: use the LLM once to emit a selector, cache the selector, demote the site back to deterministic. LLM cost amortizes across pages of the same template.

LLM extraction is **overkill** for high-volume scrapes of a single site with stable HTML, pages with existing structured data (JSON-LD, microdata, RSS, GraphQL behind the UI), and pure markdownification for RAG (use Jina Reader or Firecrawl /scrape without an extraction LLM). It is **essential** for long-tail scraping across hundreds of source domains where per-site selectors are uneconomical, fields buried in unstructured prose, frequently changing layouts, and semantic transformations like classification or entity normalization.

The signal worth tracking: **small specialist models are eating the bottom of the extraction LLM market.** Jina's ReaderLM-v2 (1.5B params, Apache 2.0) claims to outperform GPT-4o and Gemini 2.0 Flash on HTML-to-Markdown benchmarks at roughly $0 marginal cost when self-hosted. [verified on Jina's claims, plausible on independent verification] Expect more task-specific SLMs (extraction, classification) compressing margins for generic LLM-based scraping vendors. This benefits anyone shipping an extraction library that can wrap multiple model backends behind one `IChatClient`-shaped abstraction.

## The anti-bot moat has solidified, and it sits inside Cloudflare

The single most important shift in 2024 to 2026 is that detection moved from "check one signal" to **cross-validating that TLS, HTTP/2, JavaScript runtime, and behavior all agree on a single browser identity, scored by a per-customer ML model.** [high-confidence, consistent across Scrapfly, ScrapeOps, Castle.io, DataDome primary sources] Any mismatch is fatal.

Concrete state of play. **JA3 collapsed** when Chrome 110+ shipped TLS extension permutation in early 2023; a single Chrome install now produces near-infinite distinct JA3 hashes. JA4/JA4+ (FoxIO, John Althouse) replaced it, normalizing ordering and adding ALPN, SNI presence, signature algorithms. Cloudflare integrated JA4 and JA4 Signals into Bot Management in 2024 to 2025. **HTTP/2 fingerprinting (Akamai's frame ordering, settings, pseudo-header order) is harder to spoof than TLS** because most HTTP libraries do not expose these to the application layer. .NET's `HttpClient` falls in this category: cipher ordering, GREASE values, ALPN are dictated by Schannel (Windows) or OpenSSL (Linux/macOS) and not user-controllable. [verified by absence from .NET API surface]

The 2024 inflection point on the browser side was **CDP `Runtime.enable` detection**: Puppeteer, Playwright, Selenium-CDP, and undetected-chromedriver all call it, exposing a tripwire that Antoine Vastel (DataDome) publicized in June 2024 and Cloudflare, DataDome, Kasada adopted. The frontier moved from JavaScript stealth plugins (puppeteer-extra-stealth, last meaningful release March 2023, now reliably detected) to **patched runtimes** that modify the browser's compiled code so detection signals never exist in JavaScript's view: nodriver (CDP-direct, avoids Runtime.enable), Patchright (Playwright fork), Camoufox (C++ patches to Firefox internals plus Juggler protocol). Independent ScrapeOps benchmarks on 20 hard domains: Patchright and puppeteer-extra-stealth roughly 35% success, undetected-chromedriver near 0%, smart proxy APIs through aggregators near 97%. [verified]

The economic conclusion: **paid unblocking APIs are decisively eating the DIY scraping market for protected sites.** [high-confidence] Bright Data Web Unlocker, Oxylabs Web Unblocker (which acquired ScrapingBee in June 2025), ZenRows, Zyte API, Scrapfly, and ScraperAPI operate at 90% to 98% success against the hardest 15 to 20 sites in published benchmarks, where DIY stacks operate at 30% to 40%. The 60% delta on hard targets is too large to overcome with engineering. The right posture for any new library is therefore **integrate cleanly with unblockers as a swappable transport for the hard 15 to 20% of the web, ship best-in-class stealth and TLS fingerprinting for the remaining 80 to 85%.**

Cloudflare is the structural risk to flag. **July 1, 2025 ("Content Independence Day")** flipped new domains to default-deny for AI crawlers, launched Pay Per Crawl (HTTP 402 + Web Bot Auth message signatures), and deployed AI Labyrinth (poisoned synthetic content served to suspected bots). [verified] Cloudflare fronts roughly 16 to 20% of websites and a higher share of high-value content. The pay-per-crawl initiative is the first credible content-access monetization layer since paywalls; it currently targets identifiable AI bot user-agents and Web Bot Auth signed crawlers, leaving residential-proxy-with-browser-fingerprint scrapers in a gray zone. The Meta v. Bright Data ruling (January 2024, Judge Chen) and X Corp v. Bright Data (May 2024, Judge Alsup, dismissed on Copyright Act preemption of state-law contract claims) protect the legal right to scrape public data without login. The technical right is being squeezed by infrastructure-level pressure regardless. [verified]

## Headless browsers consolidate around Playwright

Playwright is the de facto winner for both deterministic scraping and as the substrate of agentic browsing. [high-confidence] Every major agent framework (browser-use at ~75k stars, Stagehand at ~22.5k, Skyvern at ~21k, Notte, Browser Use Cloud, Steel) is built on or interoperates with Playwright. Microsoft is investing heavily: Playwright MCP server, Playwright Test Agents (Planner, Generator, Healer), Azure Playwright Testing, GitHub Copilot Coding Agent integration. The 2025 release cycle (1.51 to 1.59) shipped agent-targeted features specifically: `locator.ariaSnapshot()` with bounding boxes for vision models, `expect(page).toMatchAriaSnapshot()` for resilient assertions on accessibility trees, persistent contexts, partitioned cookies. **Playwright for .NET is genuinely first-class**, tracking Node within roughly one release lag because all language bindings use the same Node-based driver. Feature parity is structurally enforced, not best-effort port. [verified]

Puppeteer is in maintenance mode for automation purposes (Chromium-only, original leads moved to Microsoft and built Playwright). Selenium is alive on enterprise inertia (~12x larger install base by company count, BiDi progressively shipping through v4.30 to 4.41) but rarely the right choice for new scraping work. The CDP-versus-BiDi protocol question resolves as: BiDi is the W3C interoperability standard with Chrome 128+ and Firefox 129+ support (no Safari yet), but **CDP remains structurally favored for scraping because Chrome dominates targets, CDP is more featureful for low-level network interception, and stealth tools (nodriver, Patchright) are built directly on CDP.** [high-confidence]

The browser-as-a-service category exploded in 2024 to 2025 driven by agentic AI demand for disposable, isolated, anti-detected browser instances. Browserbase raised a $40M Series B in June 2025 at $300M post-money, $67.5M total, 50M+ sessions in 2025, customers including Perplexity and Vercel. Pricing converges around **$0.05 to $0.12 per browser-hour** (Browserbase $0.10 to $0.12, Steel $0.05 to $0.10, Hyperbrowser ~$0.10, Cloudflare Browser Rendering $0.09, launched April 2025 as a major incumbent threat). [verified] Resource math: Chromium needs 300 to 500 MB allocated per concurrent instance with resource blocking enabled, roughly 20 to 30 instances on a 16 GB server, browser scraping is 10x to 100x more expensive per page than HTTP-only scraping with TLS impersonation. The production answer is hybrid: classify URLs, route the 60 to 80% that don't need JavaScript to HTTP with curl_cffi-class fingerprinting, route the hard ones to browsers.

On agentic browsing specifically: it is **not cannibalizing traditional scraping; it is growing the pie.** [high-confidence] Per-task cost runs $1 to $10 in LLM inference plus $0.05 to $0.12 in browser-hour, 10x to 100x more expensive than scripted scraping. Economics only work for one-off tasks, high-value per execution, or cacheable on second run (Stagehand caching, Browserbase Director, Mariner's "Teach and Repeat" approach, before Google sunset Mariner on May 4, 2026 and folded it into Gemini API). The mature pattern: **agentic browsing discovers and learns a workflow, then generates a deterministic Playwright script for repeated runs.** Browserbase's Director, Stagehand's caching, and Playwright Test Agents' Planner/Generator pair all reflect this. Reworkd's whole product is exactly this. Long-running structured scraping at scale stays deterministic; agentic dominates new use cases that were previously infeasible.

## MCP is the new integration surface

Model Context Protocol (Anthropic, November 25, 2024) became the de facto agent-tool standard within twelve months. [verified] Cursor, Windsurf, Zed, Replit (Jan to Feb 2025), OpenAI Agents SDK and ChatGPT Desktop (March 2025, replacing SSE with Streamable HTTP and adding OAuth 2.1), Google DeepMind Gemini SDK and Microsoft VS Code Agent mode plus Copilot Studio (April 2025), MCP donation to the Linux Foundation Agentic AI Foundation (late 2025). Every major LLM client speaks MCP. **Bright Data, Firecrawl, Apify, Playwright, ZenRows, Zyte, ScrapeGraphAI, and AgentQL all shipped MCP servers in 2025.** The pitch shifted from "API for your code" to "tool for your agent."

Three structural consequences for scraping tools. First, **the unit of consumption is a tool call from an LLM, not an HTTP request from a script.** Output must be LLM-ready Markdown or typed JSON with self-describing schemas, not raw HTML. Second, **discoverability beats documentation**: agents that support dynamic tool discovery auto-load relevant tools at runtime (Apify's `add-actor`, MCP registries). Third, **anti-bot is the moat**: Bright Data explicitly markets that "Web MCP wraps the entire internet" because the value is unblocking, not protocol.

For a .NET library, MCP exposure is now table stakes for agent discoverability. The official `modelcontextprotocol/csharp-sdk` is at 0.9.0-preview.1, Microsoft-collaborated, packaged as `ModelContextProtocol`, `ModelContextProtocol.AspNetCore`, `ModelContextProtocol.Core`, integrated with `IChatClient` and `AIFunction`. [verified] 1.0 GA is targeted for Q1 2026 (some breaking changes expected before then, flag this as a risk). **Nobody has shipped a scraping-as-MCP-server package in C# yet.** Open niche.

The llms.txt standard (Jeremy Howard, September 2024) is not dead but is not a working standard either. SE Ranking's November 2025 study of ~300,000 domains found 10.13% adoption with **no statistical correlation to AI citation frequency.** [verified] Adoption skews to developer-tooling docs (Anthropic, Cloudflare, Stripe, Vercel, Hugging Face); near-zero among top 1,000 sites. Major LLM crawlers do not consume it in meaningful volume. Treat as low-priority feature, not strategic.

Cloudflare's data on the training-versus-inference shift is the most useful single signal. **ChatGPT-User (live fetch on user request) grew +2,825% in raw requests from May 2024 to May 2025; GPTBot (training) grew +305%; PerplexityBot live fetch grew roughly +157,000%.** [verified] Inference and RAG scraping is the higher-growth, more defensible, lower-legal-risk segment. The Bartz settlement ($1.5B reported, August 2025) and Thomson Reuters v. Ross (February 2025, first federal rejection of fair use for AI training, currently on 3rd Circuit appeal) raised the legal exposure of bulk training scrapes sharply. Common Crawl plus FineWeb (15T tokens) plus RedPajama-V2 plus Hugging Face's broader dataset hub absorb most of the bulk-training need for general pretraining. **Build for live retrieval, not bulk training.**

## The .NET ecosystem reality

The existing libraries are healthier than the OSS narrative suggests but innovation-stagnant. [verified] HtmlAgilityPack moves 410k NuGet downloads per day, last release October 2025, but ZZZ Projects is essentially a marketing vehicle. AngleSharp at 5.5k stars released v1.4.0 in November 2025 with .NET 10 target. **PuppeteerSharp (hardkoded) is the bright spot**: very active, v24.40.0 in March 2026, AOT support since v19 in August 2024, separate `PuppeteerSharp.Cdp` package for AOT binary-size optimization, BiDi support for Firefox added in v21. Playwright for .NET tracks upstream at v1.58 (February 2026). ScrapySharp is effectively dead. Fizzler last updated April 2020. DotnetSpider stale. Even Tyrrrz's YoutubeExplode is explicitly in maintenance mode (bug fixes only), and that has been the .NET community's flagship specialty scraper.

The real gaps versus Python and Node:

| Capability | Python/Node state | .NET state |
|---|---|---|
| LLM extraction wrappers | Firecrawl, Crawl4AI, ScrapeGraphAI native | Thin SDK clients to hosted APIs only |
| TLS fingerprint control | curl_cffi, curl-impersonate, utls, tls-client | None. `HttpClient` exposes nothing |
| Browser stealth runtimes | nodriver, Camoufox, Patchright | None. Would need remote CDP to a Python sidecar |
| MCP scraping server | Several in Python and Node | Open niche (C# MCP SDK exists, no scraping wrapper shipped) |
| Agent loop primitives | browser-use, Stagehand, Skyvern | None (Microsoft Agent Framework primitives exist, not wired) |

The .NET AI stack is genuinely strong on substrate. **Microsoft.Extensions.AI is GA-stable** with `IChatClient`, `IEmbeddingGenerator<,>`, OpenTelemetry, distributed caching, function invocation middleware. **Microsoft Agent Framework was announced at .NET Conf 2025 as the consolidated successor to Semantic Kernel and AutoGen, RC at announcement, 1.0 GA targeted Q1 2026.** [verified] This is framework whiplash you must avoid: do not couple a scraping library tightly to Semantic Kernel naming; bind to `Microsoft.Extensions.AI.Abstractions` which is the durable lower layer.

**.NET 9 to 10 AOT is the most credible technical differentiator.** [verified] AWS Lambda cold start: regular .NET 8 ~6,680ms, AOT .NET 10 ~940ms, 7.1x faster, 52% less memory, 73% cost savings. SnapStart adds another 58 to 94% reduction. PuppeteerSharp already supports AOT. AngleSharp targets .NET 10. C# has source generators and `System.Text.Json` source-gen that allow strongly typed, reflection-free, AOT-friendly structured extraction that **Python cannot match structurally**, not just in implementation. This is the one place where the .NET type system advantage over Python is real and demonstrable.

On demand: Stack Overflow Developer Survey 2024 puts C# at 27.1% of all developers and **.NET is the #1 most-used non-web framework**, ahead of NumPy, Pandas, Spring. The 2025 survey showed Python adoption surging +7pp while C# held steady, confirming the AI gravity but also confirming .NET's enterprise base did not erode. .NET-heavy industries (financial services trading, insurance, healthcare back-office, government on Azure Gov, gaming backends) often run on-prem or air-gapped, have CISO policies against polyglot adds, and will pay rather than glue together OSS. That is your buyer.

## Decision framework

The credible product positioning, with three required pillars:

1. **AOT-clean pipeline end to end.** HTTP, parsing, optional embedded headless, all AOT-compileable. Single-binary or tight container for Lambda, Cloudflare Workers (via .NET), Azure Container Apps. Use the cold-start benchmark numbers as the marketing.
2. **Native MCP server plus typed extraction via `IChatClient`.** Expose scraping as MCP tools (scrape, crawl, extract-with-schema, interact) out of the box. Use source generators on `[ScrapeSchema]` POCOs so users get strongly typed, AOT-friendly, reflection-free structured extraction. This is genuinely better than Python's Pydantic plus instructor approach, not just a port.
3. **At least one real anti-bot primitive.** Either a TLS impersonation HTTP client (P/Invoke over BoringSSL or wrap bogdanfinn/tls-client Go binary) or a credible stealth headless mode. **TLS impersonation is the bigger moat and the bigger engineering risk;** it is a multi-month rabbit hole and the absence in .NET is verified. Without one of these, customers can use AngleSharp plus Playwright .NET plus a hosted unblocker and you have no reason to exist.

Two ways this fails. First, **trying to compete with Bright Data on residential proxies.** Capital-intensive, ethically charged, margin-thin. Don't. Wrap them instead. Second, **trying to match Python feature-for-feature.** Crawl4AI got to 50k stars in roughly a year; your realistic envelope is 1k to 5k stars over 2 to 3 years if you do excellent work. Do not measure success by Python's yardstick.

Dual licensing (MIT core, commercial pro tier for anti-bot bits) is the right structure. Pure OSS will not repay the effort given Python's gravity well, especially since the hard pieces (TLS fingerprint, anti-bot) cannot easily be community-maintained. Pure commercial cuts off the developer mindshare that drives enterprise procurement leads.

## What changes in the next 24 months

**Cryptographic crawler authentication (Web Bot Auth plus pay-per-crawl) becomes a parallel licensed-bot lane.** [plausible] Expect Apple, Google, major publishers, and a W3C track to formalize this. Sanctioned crawlers get clean access; unsanctioned crawlers face escalating cost. Your library should support both modes.

**HTTP/3 fingerprinting becomes real defense** as adoption rises. curl_cffi already gates HTTP/3 impersonation to a commercial paid tier. .NET supports HTTP/3 transport but not fingerprint control. The window to ship .NET HTTP/3 fingerprint impersonation as a credible product feature is open. [high-confidence]

**Browser-fork stealth becomes the open-source frontier.** [high-confidence] Camoufox's C++ patches to Firefox are widely considered the only consistently working open-source approach. More browser forks (likely Chromium variants) will appear because patching at the JavaScript layer is a losing battle. A C# library will not compete in this space; you will integrate via remote CDP.

**The 3rd Circuit ruling on Thomson Reuters v. Ross**, expected in 2026, will be the first circuit-level fair-use-for-AI ruling and will materially reshape training-data legal posture. Reddit v. Anthropic remanded to state court in September 2025 will test whether terms-of-service plus ignoring robots.txt creates contract liability against AI labs. Live retrieval / RAG remains the safer legal segment.

**Consolidation continues in unblocking.** Oxylabs acquired ScrapingBee in June 2025. Expect more. A C# library that ships with adapters for the surviving incumbents (Bright Data, Oxylabs, Zyte, Scrapfly, ZenRows, Apify, Firecrawl) at the transport layer ages well regardless of who wins.

## Conclusion

Hand-writing selectors is not dead. It is the cheap, fast, reliable substrate that every AI extraction tool sits on top of, and it remains 100x to 1000x cheaper per page than LLM extraction at scale. The 2026 production pattern is selectors first, schema-driven LLM fallback for failed validations and layout changes, vision-LLM only for visually encoded fields. **What is dead is the assumption that being a clever scraping library is enough.** The value has moved up the stack to schema-validated typed extraction, agent-callable MCP exposure, and integration with the AI runtime. And the value has moved down the stack to anti-bot infrastructure where Cloudflare's July 2025 default-deny shift made the defender side decisively richer than the attacker side.

For a C# library author in 2026, the rational moves are these. Stop thinking of this as "a scraping library." Think of it as **a .NET-native agent tool runtime with scraping as the headline use case.** Bind to Microsoft.Extensions.AI for AI substrate. Ship MCP server packaging from day one. Use source generators to deliver strongly typed structured extraction that Python structurally cannot match. Integrate with paid unblockers as transport for the hard 15 to 20% of the web. Invest seriously in one anti-bot primitive (TLS fingerprint impersonation is the largest moat). Target enterprise .NET shops where polyglot friction is a real procurement obstacle, not GitHub-star-counting hobbyists. Price commercially.

The ship sailed for the library you would have built in 2019. A different ship is leaving in 2026, and there is space on board, but only for projects that take all three of AOT, MCP-native AI integration, and anti-bot seriously. If any one of those three is more work than you want to commit to, the rational decision is to not revive, recommend AngleSharp plus Playwright .NET plus a hosted unblocking API to your customers, and put your engineering capacity somewhere with less gravity-well drag.
